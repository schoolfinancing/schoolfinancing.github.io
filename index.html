School Financing for Salt Lake Community College Students

Understanding your financial aid options can be important for your degree and for your wallet

Where to start?

Previous Next

Scholarships

Scholarships can be a great way to finance your education without breaking the bank. SLCC has a specific application process that includes most scholarships offered specific to SLCC and is affiliates.

When it comes to scholarships, it’s all about your application. The financial aid office and the scholarship help desk can help you put together the application, but the effort is up to you. You can look at this checklist to help guide you to a winning application.



FAFSA

FAFSA is the Free Application for Federal Student Aid. This application submits you for consideration for federal grants, federal loans, and work-study programs.

The FAFSA website not only walks you through your application and submits to all of the different grant and loan divisions and gives you the option for work-study, but they also have great resources for preparing for college.

With information about the different kinds of federal aid available, checklists for preparation, and resources for professional and graduate advancement, the FAFSA site can guide you through.



Private loans

Private loans, similar to federal loans, are just like they sound. This is money you have to pay back. Whether you go with federal or private loans is up to you, but it is important to get familiar with the difference between the two.

You will have to consider things like interest rates, when they will need to be repaid, credit, and many other factors that will affect how you receive your loan as well as how the loan may affect you.

Private loans, although the easiest, can be tricky. It is up to you to pay them back responsibly to avoid penalty.

SLCC Specific Options

Work Study

Work Study provides part-time employment that undergraduate students at SLCC can utilize to help finance their education. While you work to pay for your education, you can also learn more about community service, hard work, and responsibility.

Some work study options can provide employment opportunities related to your course of study, and you get the satisfaction of knowing that your tuition money was well earned.

Tuition Waivers

Tuition Waivers can be offered for many different situations. The scholarship application process will usually cover most tuition waivers as well, but specialized tuition waivers have to be applied for directly with scholarship team.

Tuition waivers can assist financially strained students who can demonstrate their need for partial or full tuition coverage, Sterling Scholars category winners and runners-up, veteran students who have been awarded a purple heart, military and police surviving dependants, and many other unique situations.
